0pen_5pace Hosts a Series of Open Mic Events in Cape Town

By Doniella Brandt on 30 December 2018

In the last couple of weeks 0pen_5pace hosted a total of four vibrant open mic events. Three of these events took place at The Peacock in Observatory, a cosy little bar just off the main road. They ended the show of with a last open mic event at the famous Trench Town (for all the weed-lovers out there). These events attracted musicians from all across the spectrum of genres who share a love for music. As someone who is a self-proclaimed music enthusiast, these events all shared a similar feeling of excitement and exposure to the vast talent we have here in Cape Town.
The first three events at The Peacock had a cosy and intimate feeling to them. There weren’t too many seats, but as the night went on you could see people sitting on the stairs or standing between the tables as they were so intrigued by the performances. There was a range of people from all races and socio- economic backgrounds coming together to just enjoy the music brought to us by the local scene. The feeling of unity throughout the night was something to cherish and a great eye-opener to know that something as simple as music could create such a sense of oneness. Throughout the nights there were a range of different artist performing covers as well as some of their own work. You could expect to hear anything from rock and folk, to hip hop, rap, R&B and soul – basically anything you can imagine. What they all shared, however, was a local vibe. There was a shared sense of knowing, between the audience members, that this is the underground sound of Cape Town and in a larger sense South Africa. Not denying the fact that these artists could easily compete on an international basis – there was a great feeling of pride in being South African in these moments.
As a great end-off to these open mic events for the year 2018, the last show took place at the exciting Trench Town. With a bit more space to entertain, it was a great way to end the year of on a bang and accommodate a much wider audience. The vibe was even greater than before, and you felt like you just didn’t want the evening the end. The drinks were flowing, the artists were even more lively than before, and those who wanted to smoke some weed to keep the fire burning, could do just that. The crowd seemed like they were having the time of their lives, with videos being taken of the artist’s performances and people just jamming together to the music and having great conversation throughout the night. As the evening came to an end, I knew that shows like these are what shapes the Cape Town culture and South Africa as a whole.
I can honestly say that I am already extremely excited for the next shows that will be taking place in 2019. I will definitely be listening to some of the music that the artists have in their oeuvre and hopefully be singing along more accurately next year. These shows are already marked as “not to be missed” on my calendar for the year. Hope to see you all there to enjoy this experience with.
